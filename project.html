Parameter Assessment for child rescue from borewell


The project “Parameter Assessment for Child Rescue from Borewell” focuses on the development of a system by employing a range of sensors and Arduino-based control mechanisms.The Arduino Uno microcontroller serves as the central processing unit, coordinating the actions of various sensors such as the ultrasonic sensor for measuring distance, PIR sensor for detecting motion, DHT11 sensor for monitoring temperature and humidity, and MQ135 sensor for detecting toxic gases. The data collected from these sensors is displayed on an LCD unit for realtime monitoring. The NRF24L01 module enables wireless communication between the rescue system and a remote monitoring unit. Through the integration of these components, the project aims to enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of child rescue operations in borewell incidents.The system is comprised of two main components: a stationary Arduino Uno part and a mobile Arduino Uno part. The stationary part functions as a receiver, while the mobile part serves as a transmitter. The transmitter Arduino is inserted into the borewell and utilizes an L298N motor driver and a 10 rpm DC motor to descend down the borewell. An ultrasonic sensor HCSR04 detects obstacles, causing the transmitter to halt at a distance of 25 cm from the obstacle and initiate the environmental observation process.
